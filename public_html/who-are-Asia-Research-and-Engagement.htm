About Asia Research & Engagement (ARE)

Benjamin McCarron - Founder and Managing Director

In 2013, Ben established his own firm, Asia Research and Engagement, to provide specialist research and representation services for institutional investors in respect of their Asian investments. Prior to this, he was Head of Research at Responsible Research, a Singapore-based consultancy, where his publication, "The Future of Fish in Asia," won the Farsight Award for best ESG/financial research in 2011. He designed and edited numerous other reports with Responsible Research, including "Sustainable Stock Exchanges II", which surveyed leading global stock exchanges on how they encourage listed issuers to provide sustainability related information.

Before moving to Asia in 2011, Ben was an analyst for The Cooperative Asset Management (now Royal London Asset Management) in the UK. He led integration of environmental and social analysis into the investment and engagement processes. He was also the lead author of two "Good Companies Guides" published in The Observer.

Ben was earlier a fund manager at Framlington, where he managed £100m of small-cap UK pension money and significantly outperformed the benchmark. He also co-managed a global technology fund covering IT and healthcare research at Gerrard, now part of Barclays Wealth.

Ben graduated with joint honours in Maths and Philosophy from University College, Oxford.

Previous publications

Sustainable Stock Exchanges Report: A Report on Progress

Commissioned by Aviva and supported by UNCTAD, Global Compact, and the PRI, this report was launched at Rio +20. NYSE Euronext subsequently joined the Sustainable Stock Exchange Initiative with a commitment to bringing in ESG disclosure by issuers on the exchanges. The report is available at www.sseinitiative.org/publications/

The Future of Fish in Asia

The David and Lucile Packard Foundation commissioned this report, which won the 2011 Farsight award for the best ESG/ Financial report of the year. It provides investors with a template for engaging companies across the Asian seafood supply chains to improve the sustainability of their production and sourcing. The report is available at www.longfinance.net/component/yuidt/index.php?option=com_content

Feeding Asia: Issues for Responsible Investors

Arisaig, a boutique investor that focuses exclusively on listed consumer businesses in emerging markets, commissioned this report on the Responsible Investment issues in Asian food supply. The report is available at chinawaterrisk.org/research-reports/feeding-asia-2/

Good Companies Guide 2010 – How sustainability affects business

This report, published by the Observer, looked at the FTSE 350 from ecological and societal perspectives. It considered for each company whether related issues were likely to present opportunities or risks and how strongly.

Good Companies Guide 2009 – Gender balance in Britain plc.

This report, published by the Observer, ranked FTSE 350 companies on gender balance. It considered the board composition as well as the depth and breadth of equal opportunities related disclosure.
